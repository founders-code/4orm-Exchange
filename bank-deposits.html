What is a tokenized deposit receipt?

A tokenized deposit receipt is a permissioned token issued 1:1 against a Canadian dollar held at a deposit-taking institution (illustrative). It moves on the 4orm-EVM permissioned network with the same legal characteristics as the underlying deposit — [deposit insurance not represented] applies at the bank, and redemption is by delivery-versus-payment (illustrative). Unlike a stablecoin, the underlying is a regulated bank liability, not a money-market fund or commercial-paper basket.

On 4ormEx the tokenized deposit is the designed cash leg for delivery-versus-payment. Buyers and sellers settle in tokenized CAD at T+0 instead of waiting for CDS T+2. The token IS the payment — there's no separate wire to reconcile.

Institution-issued (illustrative) tokenized deposits are differentiated from stablecoins under OSFI's October 2025 framework because the underlying is the bank's own deposit liability, not a money-market reserve basket. This brings them into the bank's existing prudential perimeter rather than requiring separate Group 2 capital treatment — and gives investors the same [deposit-insurance regime · not represented] protection that applies to the underlying deposit.
