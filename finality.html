Settlement finality

Final at commit. Not eventually. Not probably.

Settlement finality is a property of the layer, not a property of speed. A fast settlement is not the same as a final settlement. 4ormEx is designed so that the moment of match is the moment of commit, and the moment of commit is the moment of finality.

Finality is a property. Not a feature.

Three things have to be true at the same time for a trade to be final.

1. The asset leg has transferred.

The canonical ledger reflects the new holder of the token. The prior holder cannot transfer the same token again.

2. The cash leg has transferred.

The tokenized commercial-bank deposit is now the new holder's liability claim against the bank. The prior holder's claim is extinguished.

3. The custody record has updated.

The qualified Canadian custodian has acknowledged the new holding. Reporting, tax, and supervisory records derive from this acknowledgement.

If any of the three would fail, the commit does not happen, and none of the three legs change. There is no intermediate state where one leg has moved and the others have not.

The sequence.

Three events. One outcome.

T+0 is the settlement target. Finality is at commit, not at T+0 itself.

Finality versus reconciliation.

Reconciliation

  • Records on multiple systems.
  • A periodic process to reconcile them.
  • A window during which records disagree.
  • Errors discovered in arrears.
  • A reconciliation team and a reconciliation workflow.

Finality at commit

  • One canonical record.
  • No reconciliation process required at the layer.
  • No window during which records disagree.
  • A failed commit fails the whole transaction.
  • No reconciliation team and no reconciliation workflow at the layer level.
