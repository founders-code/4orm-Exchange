About 4ormEx

A regulated trading venue, one pillar of six.

4ormEx is the planned permissioned trading venue (Pillar 02) inside the 4orm Finance regulated control plane. The other five pillars — Issuance, Settlement, Custody, Reporting, and Supervision — operate at the platform level under 4orm Finance Holdings Inc. This page explains what that means, what 4ormEx is and is not, and how the corporate structure is organized.

What 4ormEx is, and is not

4ormEx is a design preview of the planned permissioned trading venue (Pillar 02 of six) inside the future 4orm Finance regulated control plane. Today, 4ormEx is software. It is not a registered marketplace, dealer, exchange, money services business, or bank in any Canadian jurisdiction. No applications have been filed.

Operating a marketplace for securities is a registrable activity in Canada under National Instruments 21-101, 23-101, 24-102 and 31-103. 4ormEx intends to seek marketplace recognition and dealer registration before operating any live trading. Until that registration exists, no order, custody, deposit, or payment is being offered, accepted, or solicited through this site.

Every figure, identifier, price, balance, hash, and counterparty shown anywhere on this site is simulated and illustrative. No real institutions are named anywhere. Where an entity is referenced, it is described by category only. Nothing on this site is an offer to sell or solicitation to buy securities, crypto assets, deposits, or any financial instrument, and nothing on this site is financial, legal, tax, or investment advice. Questions: compliance@4ormfinance.com.

The six pillars of the 4orm control plane

Where 4ormEx sits

The regulated control plane operates a perimeter inside which Canadian institutions can issue, trade, settle, custody, report, and supervise tokenized real-world assets. Each capability is a pillar. The canonical ledger of record lives at the platform level. 4ormEx is the trading pillar.

Pillar 01

Issuance

EMD-governed primary issuance, transfer rules embedded at mint, distribution scope set per NI 45-106.

Operated by 4orm OpCo (sibling)
Pillar 02

Trading

Permissioned marketplace. Eligibility check before order match. Institutional members only. CIRO member firm target.

Settlement

Operated at platform level

FINTRAC, CSA, OSFI reporting derived from the canonical ledger. No standalone filing surface at any single OpCo.

Operated at platform level

Supervision

Read-only feed to OSFI / CSA / FINTRAC / BoC / FSRA / AMF. Privacy-preserving aggregate; never reads core banking.

Operated at platform level
Corporate structure

Who builds it, who owns it, who runs each pillar

KCS Capital is the independent engineering firm that designs and builds the platform technology. KCS is not the parent of 4orm Finance. 4orm Finance Holdings Inc. is the parent legal entity, sitting above three operating siblings.

Engineering firm · independent
KCS Capital

Designs and builds the platform technology under contract. Not a parent. Not regulated. Receives no consolidated control of the platform's regulated entities.

Parent · platform holding company
4orm Finance Holdings Inc.

Owns and governs the three operating entities. Operates the canonical ledger, the compliance engine, and the supervisory feed at the platform level. Independent governance from KCS.

Sibling · marketplace operator
4orm OpCo

CSA-recognized marketplace operator. Pillar 01 (Issuance) governance. Handles primary issuance flow and issuer onboarding under EMD rules.

Sibling · exchange operator
4ormEx OpCo

This site. Planned CIRO member firm. Pillar 02 (Trading) operator. Operates the permissioned trading venue, matching engine, and pre-trade eligibility checks. Not yet registered.

Sibling · qualified custodian
4orm Trust Co

Qualified digital asset custodian. CIRO Custody Framework Tier-2 target. Pillar 04 (Custody) operator. Holds the on-chain receipts for institutional buyers.

What the platform owns vs. integrates

Provider neutrality

The platform owns the ledger of record, the compliance engine, the settlement orchestration, the issuer governance, and the supervisory feed. Everything else is an adapter. That posture is how 4orm stays provider-neutral and regulator-aligned at the same time.

Owned (never delegated)

  • · Canonical ledger of record
  • · Compliance engine
  • · Settlement orchestration
  • · Treasury coordination
  • · Issuer governance framework
  • · Token registry
  • · Supervisory auditability surface

Integrated (bounded, swappable)

  • · Underlying chains (Ethereum, Stellar; Hyperledger reference)
  • · Interop fabric (cross-chain bridges, message buses)
  • · Qualified custodians (multiple Canadian options, sibling Trust Co as anchor)
  • · KYC and AML providers (multiple)
  • · Payment rails (ISO 20022, Lynx, ACSS, RTR)
  • · Cash-leg tokens (VRCAs, tokenized deposit receipts, future CBDC)
  • · Identity attestations (W3C verifiable credentials)
